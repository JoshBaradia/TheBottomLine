Our problem statement

Ocean plastic pollution poses a significant environmental crisis, with far-reaching impacts on marine ecosystems, human health, and the global economy. Each year, millions of tons of plastic waste enter the world's oceans, accumulating in vast garbage patches, washing up on shores, and infiltrating even the deepest ocean trenches. This relentless influx of plastic debris threatens marine life, damages fragile ecosystems, and undermines the health and resilience of ocean ecosystems.

What do we do?

As per the regulations issued in Plastic Waste Management Rules 2016 all producers, brand owners, resellers, importers manufacturers of plastic are liable for safe disposal post-consumer use or 'end of life'. Thus, to ensure the right disposal is achieved producers may collaborate with Waste Management Agency (WMA) in order to establish a distinctive collection channel for their Plastic Waste. This is further done by WMA's tie ups with Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) / Municipalities.
